OSI Reference Model

A seven-layer structure designed to describe computer network architectures and teh way that data passes through them. This model was developed by the ISO in 1978 to clearly define the interfaces in multivendor networks, and to provide users of those networks with conceptual guidelines in the construction of such networks. See also International Organization for Standardization. (From RFC1983.)
