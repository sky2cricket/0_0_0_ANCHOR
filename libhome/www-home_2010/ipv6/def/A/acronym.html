acronym

The creation of a new word (usually a noun) by taking the initials of a phrase or some letters from a phrase to mean the same thing as the phrase. For example: IETF is the acronym for Internet Engineering Task Force.

DOD acronym directory This is the mother of all acronym pages. Unfortuately, DOD uses whatever letters it wants and has no respect for other people's acronyms! For example, IO, which has been used by the computer community for "input output" for over 30 years is defined by DOD as standing for "information objectives, intelligence oversight, and international organizations. Another example, OSI stands for Operational subsystem interface. But if you are trying to decode some government document (such as a grant application!!!), this site might give you some clues as to the meaning of the alphabet soup.
