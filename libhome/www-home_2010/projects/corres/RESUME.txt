 Experience: 
   
   
   
   
   
   
   
         1969 to 1983 :     at Tennessee Valley Authority, as a geologist and computer 
         systems-analyst. I made maps, developed databases, edited a 
         newsletter, wrote Fortran programs, helped with public information 
         requests, helped with environmental impact statements, wrote an 
         information handbook about ground water heat pumps in the TVA 
         Region, and worked on geological aspects of numerous projects. 
         I also took numerous short courses at TVA on computer operations. 
           
   
         1983 to 1994 :     full time mother, Brownie Troop Leader, Jr Pro Basketball 
         Coach, and in 1986 received Romance Writer's of America award for best 
         book length fiction by unpublished author, category "inspirational romance". 
           
   
         1995 to 1998 :     pursuit of master's degree in Computer Science. Helped 
         develop CS100 introductory course with online tools, plus wrote the 
         CS100 Lab Manual [over 100 pages]. In 1997 received DuPont award for 
         Excellence in Teaching. 
           
   
         1998 to 1999 :     Research Associate at Innovative Computing Laboratories, 
         University of Tennessee, Knoxville. Java, C, Java Native Interface, 
         hardware performance, code optimization, web page development. 
           
   
         1999 to 2000:     Web and Database programmer at Ntown.com, Knoxville. 
         Perl, Javascript, HTML, MSaccess97, SQL6.5 
         team work with management, artists, system adminstrators 
         optimization, web page development. 
           
   
         2000 to current:     Computer Systems Analyst/Webmaster for 
         the Community Health and Research Group 
         at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. 
         Coordinate efforts of statisticians, geographers, and health 
         researchers to create a user-friendly web-site to access and 
         present health data for the State of Tennessee.
