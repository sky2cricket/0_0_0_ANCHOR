NON - SEQUENTIAL FILES

o SIMPLE LINEAR FILES NOT SEQUENCED BY ANY FIELD .

o AN EXAMPLE . . .

oo CHRONOLOGICAL "PILE" IN WHICH EVERY TIME SOME NEW DATA OR RECORD IS RECEIVED . . .

> IT IS PLUNKED DOWN AT THE END OF THE FILE . .

>> THE DATA IS IN "ARRIVAL SEQUENCE" .

o NON - SEQUENTIAL FILES HAVE ONE ADVANTAGE . . .

oo NO TIME OR EFFORT IS SPENT TO MAINTAIN THE FILE IN PHYSICAL SEQUENCE . . .

> BY MERGING , SORTING , OR OTHER MANIPULATION . . .

>> EITHER WHEN THE FILE IS FIRST CREATED . .

>> OR LATER ON WHEN NEW RECORDS ARE ADDED AT THE END OF THE FILE .

o CAN NON - SEQUENTIAL FILES SERVE A PURPOSE ? . . .

oo CAN THE RECORDS FROM THE FILES BE READ IN SEQUENCE . . .

> BASED ON THE VALUES OF ONE OR MORE FIELDS ? .

oo IT MAY SOUND CONTRADICTORY . . .

> BUT MECHANISMS ARE AVAILABLE FOR DIRECTLY ACCESSING SUCH RECORDS .

INDEXED FILES

o THERE ARE LEGITIMATE REASONS FOR WANTING TO STORE RECORDS . . .

oo IN SIMPLE LINEAR FILES. . .

> AND NOT IN PHYSICAL SEQUENCE BY ANY FIELD WHILE . . .

>> (1) EXPECTING TO BE ABLE TO ACCESS THE RECORDS DIRECTLY AND . . .

>> (2) EXPECTING TO BE ABLE TO RETRIEVE THOSE RECORDS IN SEQUENCE BY SOME FIELD . . .

. WHETHER OR NOT THE FILE IS STORED IN PHYSICAL SEQUENCE BY THAT OR OTHER MEANS.

o THE PRINCIPLE USED IS THE SAME AS AN INDEX OF A BOOK . . .

oo HOW DO YOU FIND THE LOCATION OF SOME SPECIFIC PIECE OF MATERIAL IN A BOOK ?? . . .

> YOU DO NOT PAGE THROUGH THE ENTIRE BOOK UNTIL YOU FIND THE ITEM . .

> YOU LOOK IN THE INDEX AND FIND THE LOCATION OF THE ITEM IN THE BOOK.

> WE NORMALLY USE AN ALPHABETIC SEARCH THROUGH THE

INDEX . . .

>> TO FIND A POINTER TO ITS LOCATION .

SOME CONCEPTUAL CHARACTERISTICS OF INDEXES

o AN INDEX CAN BE BUILT BASED ON ANY FIELD OF A FILE . . .

oo WHETHER OR NOT THE FILE IS IN PHYSICAL SEQUENCE BASED ON THAT OR ANY OTHER FIELD .

o AN INDEX CAN BE BUILT ON A COMBINATION OF FIELDS . . .

oo EXAMPLE : EMPLOYEE LAST NAME AND EMPLOYEE FIRST NAME .

o ANY INDEX CAN BE USED BOTH FOR DIRECT ACCESS BASED ON THE SEQUENCE ON THE INDEXED FIELD . . .

oo AND FOR SEQUENTIAL RETRIEVAL OF RECORDS BASED ON THE SEQUENCE OF THE FIELD .

o MANY SEPARATE INDEXES INTO A FILE CAN EXIST SIMULTANEOUSLY . . .

oo EACH BASED ON A DIFFERENT FIELD OR A COMBINATION OF FIELDS OF THAT FILE . . .

> THE INDEXES ARE INDEPENDENT OF EACH OTHER .

o THE INDEXES MUST BE UPDATED . . .

oo WHEN CHANGES ARE MADE TO THE FILE .

oo EXAMPLE : IF ONE OF THE FIELDS IN ONE OF THE RECORDS IN THE

FILE CHANGES . . .

> AND THERE IS AN INDEX BUILT ON THAT FILE . . .

>> THEN THE INDEX FILE MUST BE UPDATED .

oo THE CHANGES COULD INCLUDE INSERTION / DELETION OF RECORDS , CHANGING A FIELD , ETC. . . .

> WHEN CHANGES ARE MADE THE INDEX FILE MUST ACTUALLY BE RESORTED WITH THE NEW DATA . . .

>> EITHER ON THE SPOT OR AT SOME LATER TIME.

> THE UPDATES MUST BE KEPT IN A LIST . . .

>> WHICH MUST BE ACCESSED IN CASE A RETRIEVAL MUST BE DONE BASED ON ONE OF THE NEW ENTRIES . . .

. BEFORE THE INDEX IS RESORTED .
