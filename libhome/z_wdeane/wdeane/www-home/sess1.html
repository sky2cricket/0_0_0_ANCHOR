o WHAT IS A DATABASE ?

oo A COLLECTION OF STORED OPERATIONAL DATA . . .

> USED BY APPLICATION PROGRAMS . .

> AND FOR A PARTICULAR COMPANY, ENTERPRISE, OR INDIVIDUAL.

oo PROVIDES A USER THE ABILITY TO PERFORM A VARIETY OF OPERATIONS ON A FILE . . .

> ADDING NEW (EMPTY) FILES TO THE DB . .

> INSERTING NEW DATA INTO EXISTING FILES . .

> RETRIEVING DATA FROM EXISTING FILES . .

> UPDATING DATA IN EXISTING FILES . .

> DELETING DATA FROM EXISTING FILES . .

> REMOVING EXISTING FILES (EMPTY OR NOT) FROM THE DATABASE.

CS-460 - Session 1

o DEFINE "DATA DEPENDENCE" AND "DATA INDEPENDENCE".

oo DATA DEPENDENCE . . .

> DATA ORGANIZED IN SECONDARY STORAGE TO BE ACCESSED IN A SPECIFIC WAY BY THE APPLICATION IN QUESTION . .

> IMPOSSIBLE TO CHANGE THE STORAGE STRUCTURE . .

> EXAMPLE (MOUSE PROGRAM).

oo DATA INDEPENDENCE . . .

> DIFFERENT APPLICATIONS NEED DIFFERENT VIEWS OF THE DATA . .

> THE DBA MUST HAVE THE FREEDOM TO CHANGE THE STORAGE STRUCTURE . . .

>> OR THE ACCESS STRATEGY TO MEET CHANGING REQUIREMENTS.

CS-460 - Session 2

o SOME DEFINITIONS RELATED TO STORED DATA . . .

oo STORED FIELD

> SMALLEST UNIT OF STORED DATA . .

> EXAMPLES: PART NUMBER, KIND OF PART, ETC.

oo STORED RECORD

> NAME OF A COLLECTION OF STORED FIELDS . .

> A GROUP OF RELATED STORED FIELD OCCURRENCES . .

> EXAMPLE IN "PARTS" DATABASE:

>> PART_NUMBER

>> PART_NAME

>> PART_COLOR

>> PART_WEIGHT

oo STORED FILE . . .

> A NAMED COLLECTION OF ALL OCCURRENCES OF ALL TYPES OF STORED RECORDS.

CS-460 - Session 2

o WHAT DOES IT MEAN TO SAY THAT A DATABASE SYSTEM IS RELATIONAL ???

oo THE DATA IS PERCEIVED BY THE USER AS TABLES . .

oo OPERATORS USED FOR DATA RETRIEVAL ARE AT THE USERS' DISPOSAL . . .

> NEW "TABLES" CAN BE GENERATED FROM OLD ONES.

oo A SIMPLE EXAMPLE:

> ASSUME FIELDS OF STORED RECORDS ARE:

(PART_NUMBER, COLOR), (PART_NUMBER, WEIGHT)

> THESE CAN BE COMBINED TO GIVE A TABLE WHOSE RECORDS HAVE FIELDS (PART_NUMBER, COLOR, WEIGHT) ..

>> WHICH CAN BE BROKEN DOWN TO TWO TABLES HAVING RECORDS WITH FIELDS:

(PART_NUMBER, COLOR)

(PART_NUMBER, WEIGHT).

o WHAT ARE THE THREE LEVELS OF ARCHITECTURE OF DATABASE SYSTEMS ???

oo INTERNAL LEVEL

> THE ONE CLOSEST TO THE PHYSICAL LEVEL . .

> THE ONE CONCERNED WITH HOW DATA ARE ACTUALLY STORED ON A STORAGE UNIT . .

>> FILES, RECORDS, ETC.

oo EXTERNAL LEVEL

> THE ONE CLOSEST TO THE USER . .

> CONCERNED WITH THE WAY THE DATA IS VIEWED

BY INDIVIDUAL USERS . .

> VIEWS.

oo CONCEPTUAL LEVEL

> "LEVEL OF INDIRECTION" BETWEEN THE OTHER TWO . .

> CAN BE REGARDED AS CONCERNED WITH A COMMUNITY USER VIEW.

CS-460 - Session 2

o THE DATABASE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM . . .

oo SOFTWARE THAT HANDLES ALL ACCESSES TO THE DATABASE . . .

> USER ISSUES AN ACCESS REQUEST, USING SOME PARTICULAR DATA SUB-LANGUAGE (E.G., SQL) . .

> THE DBMS INTERCEPTS THE REQUEST AND ANALYZES IT . .

> THE DBMS INSPECTS THE FOLLOWING:

>> EXTERNAL SCHEMA FOR THAT USER,

>> THE CORRESPONDING EXTERNAL/CONCEPTUAL MAPPING,

>> THE CONCEPTUAL SCHEMA,

>> THE CONCEPTUAL/INTERNAL MAPPING,

>>

THE STORAGE STRUCTURE DEFINITION.

> THE DBMS EXECUTES THE NECESSARY OPERATIONS ON THE DATABASE.
