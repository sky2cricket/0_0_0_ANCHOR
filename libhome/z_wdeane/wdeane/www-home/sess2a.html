SECONDARY MEMORY

o DUE TO THE SCARCITY OF PRIMARY MEMORY . . .

oo VAST VOLUMES OF DATA AND PROGRAMS THAT PROCESS THE DATA . . .

> ARE MUST BE STORED ON SECONDARY MEMORY DEVICES (E.G. MAG -

NETIC DISKS) .

o INDIVIDUAL RECORDS OR GROUPS OF RECORDS OF A FILE AND OF PROGRAMS . . .

oo ARE LOADED FROM PRIMARY MEMORY TO SECONDARY MEMORY

WHEN REQUIRED FOR PROCESSING.

o THERE ARE FUNDAMENTALLY ONLY TWO KINDS OF ACCESS TO STORED DATA . . .

oo REGARDLESS OF THE NUMBER OF VARIATIONS OF PROCESSING APPLICATIONS . . .

> SEQUENTIAL ACCESS . .

> DIRECT ACCESS (DASD) .

o SEQUENTIAL : ALL OR A PORTION OF THE RECORD OF A FILE . . .

oo MUST BE RETRIEVED ONE AFTER ANOTHER . . .

> UNTIL THE DESIRED RECORD IS FOUND . . .

>> OR UNTIL ALL RECORDS HAVE BEEN PROCESSED .

oo EXAMPLE OF SEQUENTIAL PROCESSING . . .

> IN A COMPANY ALL EMPLOYEES MUST BE PAID WEEKLY . .

> THE APPLICATION PROGRAM PLOWS THROUGH ALL OF THE EMPLOYEES' PAYROLL FILES . . .

>> ONE AFTER ANOTHER UNTIL A PAYROLL CHECK IS GENERATED FOR EVERY EMPLOYEE .

> ORDERING THE RECORDS BY PAYROLL NUMBER . . .

>> WOULD HELP IN FINDING, ACCESSING, AND UPDATING ONE OR MORE RECORDS .

o FOUR MAJOR STEPS OR TIMING CONSIDERATIONS IN THE TRANSFER OF

DATA . . .

oo (1) SEEK TIME : MOVING THE ACCESS ARM ASSEMBLY TO THE CORRECT CYLINDER FROM THE PRESENT POSITION (THE GREATEST DELAY) . .

oo (2) HEAD SWITCHING : SELECTING THE READ / WRITE HEAD THAT ACCESSES THE PARTICULAR TRACK OF THE CYLINDERS ON WHICH THE DATA RESIDES (VIRTUALLY ZERO TIME) .

oo (3) ROTATIONAL DELAY : WAITING FOR THE DESIRED DATA TO ARRIVE UNDER THE READ / WRITE HEAD AS THE DISK PACK SPINS (ON AVERAGE, ONE - HALF OF THE ROTATIONAL TIME ).

oo (4) TRANSFER TIME : THE TIME TO ACTUALLY MOVE THE DATA FROM THE DISK TO PRIMARY MEMORY ONCE STEPS 1 - 3 HAVE BEEN COMPLETED (RATHER FAST, DEPENDING ON THE SPEED OF THE INTERFACES ) .

o IF ALL DATA ARE STORED INITIALLY BY FILLING ALL TRACKS ON ONE CYLINDER . . .

oo ALL DATA CAN BE RETRIEVED WITHOUT HAVING TO MOVE THE ACCESS ARM.
