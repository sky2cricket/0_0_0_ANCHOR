JOB TITLE: Computer Information Specialist (Level 9)

JOB SUMMARY: Consult with University of Tennessee customers to analyze computer-related problems, determine and communicate solutions to customers, provide answers to questions, refer problems to second-level support as necessary, maintain records, perform supervisory duties in the Coordinator's absence, and coordinate day-to-day activities.

POSITION RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Provide daily coordination of call duty
  • Provide front-line customer support over the phone, through electronic mail, and walk-in assistance
  • Consult with customers to obtain problem details
  • Coordinate access to solutions for customers and instruct them in appropriate methods of problem correction
  • Work with other DII staff to find solutions
  • Follow up with customers on solutions; follow up with DII staff on referred problems
  • Document problems and solutions in problem tracking system for reference and reporting
  • Suggest changes to policies and procedures to improve customer support
  • Assist with the development and maintenance of the Technical Support team's web pages
  • Supervise the student assistant's in the Coordinator's absence
  • Training
KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, ABILITIES
Education:
  • Bachelor's degree or equivalent experience
Personal:
  • Excellent interpersonal skills and customer service skills
  • Ability to deal with difficult situations professionally
  • Excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Ability to deal with diverse, rapidly changing situations
Technical:
  • Knowledge of Unix, PC and Mac environments
  • Knowledge of communications and networking protocols such as ftp, Fetch, WS-FTP, telnet
  • Knowledge of MS Office Suite, POP and IMAP e-mail clients
  • Knowledge of Internet browsers, virus protection,
  • Knowledge of World Wide Web use and Web page development
  • Knowledge or familiarity of Remedy ARS system preferred

 

Position Announcement

Senior Systems Analyst (Group Leader)

The University of Tennessee, Knoxville, the State’s flagship Research I institution, seeks a dynamic, service-oriented professional with an exemplary record of leadership to serve as Senior Systems Analyst Group Leader of Student Information Systems (SIS). The individual selected will lead and direct all centralized SIS development and implementation projects for the University.

The position reports to the Associate Director of Information Systems within Computing and Academic Services (CAS). CAS is an organizational unit within the Office of the Vice Chancellor for Information Infrastructure. Please refer to additional information about the Division of Information Infrastructure, including links to Computing and Academic Services and UTK, at http://www.admin.utk.edu/vcii/.

Qualifications:

  1. Proven track record and history of increasing responsibility for planning, leading, organizing, staffing, and controlling an information technology-based organization including skills in team building and facilitating group processes.
  2. Proven managerial experience in complex, long-term projects to implement high quality software applications, preferably including experience with vendor-provided applications software packages for use in Higher Education.
  3. A thorough knowledge of contemporary computing and information systems technology, including relational database technology, client/server computing, database-web integration, distributed processing, n-tier development and software implementation in a mixed platform environment.
  4. Experience with relational database technology from industry leading vendors. Experience with Oracle, Microsoft, IBM and SyBase products preferred.
  5. Experience in establishing project schedules, developing work plans, managing tasks, providing progress reports, taking initiatives to meet deadlines and mentoring team members.
  6. A basic knowledge of university operational procedures and future trends in educational technology preferred.
  7. Excellent interpersonal, communications and presentation skills necessary for working collaboratively within a large diverse university environment.

A Bachelor’s degree required, Master’s degree preferred. Salary will be commensurate with education and experience. Applicants should submit a cover letter that includes a statement of the strengths the candidate believes he/she will bring to the job, a resume, and names and contact information for three references.
