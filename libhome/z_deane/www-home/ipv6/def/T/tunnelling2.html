tunneling

IP packets can travel through other protocol areas if the entire packet (header plus data) is treated as 100% data and encapsulated within local packet protocols. In this way, IPv6 packets can travel through IPv4 segments of the networks. The process is called tunneling. In some cases tunneling is performed through same protocol networks (i.e. tunneling IPv4 packets through an IPv4 network).

More information at:

RFC2107: Ascent Tunnel Management Protocol -- ATMP.

RFC2004: Minimal Encapsulation within IP, October 1996.

RFC2003: IP Encapsulation within IP, October 1996.

RFC1972: A Method for Transmission of IPv6 Packets over Ethernet Networks, August 1996.
