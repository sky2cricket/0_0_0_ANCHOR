circuit switching

A communications paradigm in which a dedicated communication path is established between two hosts, and on which all packets travel. The telephone system is an example of a circuit switched network. See also: connection-oriented, connectionless, packet switching. (From RFC1983.)

A method of communicating in which a dedicated communications path is established between the two devices through one or more intermediate switching nodes. Unlike packet switching, digital data are sent as a continuous stream of bits. Data rate is guarenteed, and delay is essentially limited to propagation time.
