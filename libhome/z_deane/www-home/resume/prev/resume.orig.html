Work Experience:

From 1969 to 1983 I worked at the Tennessee Valley Authority as a geologist and computer systems-analyst. I made maps, developed databases, edited a newsletter, wrote Fortran programs, helped with public information requests, helped with environmental impact statements, wrote an information handbook about solar power in the TVA Region, and worked on geological aspects of numerous projects.

During my time at TVA, I also took classes at UTK in geology, geochemistry, geophysics, inorganic chemistry, statistics. I took numerous in-house short courses at TVA on computer operations and IBM 360 system programming. I also attended a week long work shop in geohydrology and ground water flow modelling at Princeton in 1980.

I retired in 1983 to raise my two daughters.

I returned to school in 1995 to pursue a master's degree in computer science. I have been a teaching assistant in cs102, cs100, and cs360. I wrote the CS100 Guide to How to Make your own Homepage (link given below) and the CS100 Lab Manual (about 80 pages) and created the web site for the course. In summer 1996, I was promoted to "valued TA" position to supervise other TA's in CS100. In spring 1997, I received the DuPont award for excellence in Teaching Assistance.

In August 1998, I was hired by the Innovative Computing Laboratories, University of Tennessee, Knoxville. I have worked on applying the Java Native Interface to the ATLAS suite of programs and to Legacy Fortran code. I am also involved with the Performance Tools Application Implementation [PAPI] project.

Johnson,R.W., Haygood,C., and Kunselman, 1980, "Residual Total Intensity Aeromagnetic Map of Kentucky: (Eastern Sheet, Middle Sheet, Western Sheet)", (3 map series), Kentucky Geological Survey, University of Kentucky, Lexington, Kentucky.

 
I developed the database to manage data collection, helped reduce field tapes of magnetic values to computer input, helped reduce field video tapes of flight path to computer input, supervised coop students in data reduction, contoured the maps, and coordinated final copy with photoshop.

 

Black,F.B., Keller,G.R., Johnson,R.W., Bland, A.E., Graham,G.M., Greenburg, J.K., Haygood,C., Kunselman, P.M., 1977, "Structural Geology, Bouguer Gravity, and Aeromagnetic Intensity for a Portion of Central Kentucky" (4 map series), Kentucky Geological Survey, University of Kentucky, Lexington, Kentucky.

 
This was an immense project that spanned several years. I developed the database that made the unification of all this data possible. I contoured all the data, helped with reduction of gravity and aeromagnetic data, and rectified three different coordinate systems (Kentucky Carter Coordinates, standard USGS latititude and longitude, and Universal Transverse Mercator grid) to a single norm for publication. When the lead cartographer died, I took over his responsibilities to coordinate final copy with photoshop.

 

"Prospects" a newsletter for the Mineral Resources Community. For one year (1978) I wrote articles, solicted articles, edited, and helped prepare final copy for "Prospects." The publication came out quarterly and was popular reading for those who cared about the comings and goings of Mineral Resoures in the seven state TVA Region. Unfortunately, President Carter invented zero-based budgeting and that took care of my career as a newsletter editor.
